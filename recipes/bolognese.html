🍝 Bolognese

Slow-cooked beef ragu built on layered flavour and proper technique.

Quick Stats

⏱ 1–3 hrs • 💪 High Protein • 🍽 4–6 servings

Ingredients

  • Beef mince
  • 1 onion (finely diced)
  • 1 carrot (finely diced)
  • 3–4 garlic cloves
  • Tomato paste
  • Canned tomatoes
  • White wine
  • Milk (small amount)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt & pepper

Method

  1. Brown the beef:
    Heat a large pan over medium-high heat with olive oil. Add beef mince and break it apart. Cook until well browned and slightly caramelised. Remove and set aside.
  2. Build the base:
    In the same pan, add onion and carrot. Cook on medium heat for 8–10 minutes until soft and slightly sweet.
  3. Add garlic:
    Add garlic and cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant. Stir constantly.
  4. Cook tomato paste:
    Add tomato paste and cook for 2–3 minutes until it darkens and becomes rich in flavour.
  5. Deglaze:
    Pour in white wine and scrape the bottom of the pan to lift all flavour bits.
  6. Build sauce:
    Add canned tomatoes and return beef to the pan. Stir and bring to a gentle simmer.
  7. Slow cook:
    Simmer uncovered for 1–3 hours. Stir occasionally and add water if needed.
  8. Finish:
    Add a small splash of milk near the end. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Nutrition

High protein, iron-rich, slow-digesting carbs when served with pasta.

Micronutrients

Iron (beef), vitamin A (carrot), antioxidants (tomatoes), immune support (garlic).

Tips

Let it rest overnight — flavour improves massively after sitting.
